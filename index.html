Elijah Guptill

Hello, my name is Elijah Guptill and I am a blended Bachelors and Masters in mathematics student at California Polytechnic State University. I have varied interests in mathematics, including, functional analysis, operator theory, harmonic analysis, and persistent homology.

I have been funded by the Frost Fund within the California Polytechnic University Bailey College of Science and Mathematics to research the computation of Legendrian Contact Homology, the continuity of mapping evaluations of sequences into Lexicographic orders measuring repetition, moduli spaces of triangles, and the complex dimensions of modified Sierpiński carpets with, Austin Christian, Ryan-Tully Doyle, Eric Brussel, and Sean Watson, respectively.

My senior undergraduate project was advised by Elena Dimitrova, and my Master's thesis is being advised by Ryan-Tully Doyle.

I intend to come to back this work, formalize its notions of substructures, extend the counting argument to mxn modified Sierpiński carpets, and update the associated program to work for mxn carpets. I hope to use connected components in a Cartesian product of finite words in a finite alphabet to make this possible.
